\begin{abstract}
Serverless platforms offer scalability and pay-per-use economics, but they remain poorly suited for stateful and long-running applications. To address this limitation, AWS recently introduced durable functions for AWS Lambda, enabling stateful execution through checkpoint-and-replay mechanisms.

This paper presents a practical investigation of AWS Lambda durable functions through progressively complex applications. We first study a stateful counter to evaluate ordering guarantees, fault tolerance, and deterministic replay. We then explore a video encoding pipeline that orchestrates parallel tasks and manages workflow state across failures. For both cases, we compare durable execution against traditional serverless designs based on explicit external state management.

Our evaluation, conducted through local execution and controlled simulations, shows that durable functions significantly reduce coordination complexity while preserving correctness and fault tolerance. Finally, we analyze durable functions through the lens of the actor model, highlighting their alignment with actor-like semantics in serverless environments and discussing their limitations. These findings position durable functions as a promising abstraction for stateful serverless systems.
\end{abstract}
